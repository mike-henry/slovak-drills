# Verb Class: -ať (AT Verbs)

<section id="verb-overview">

AT‑verbs are one of the most common and regular Slovak verb classes.  
Their infinitive ends in **‑ať**, and they form the present tense by removing **‑ať** and adding predictable endings.

Examples include:

- čakať — to wait
- hľadať — to search
- pýtať (sa) — to ask
- skladať — to assemble

This class is highly regular and forms a reliable foundation for learners.

</section>

<section id="verb-recognition">

### How to recognise AT‑verbs

A verb belongs to this class if:

- its infinitive ends in **‑ať**, and
- removing **‑ať** leaves a stable stem.

Examples:

- čakať → čak‑
- hľadať → hľad‑
- pýtať → pýt‑

</section>

<section id="verb-stem">

### How to derive the stem

The rule is straightforward:

1. Remove **‑ať**
2. The remaining part is the stem
3. No consonant is added

Examples:

- čakať → čak‑
- hľadať → hľad‑
- pýtať → pýt‑

Softening is rare and predictable.

</section>

<section id="verb-endings">

### Present‑tense endings for AT‑verbs

- **ja:** ‑ám
- **ty:** ‑áš
- **on/ona/ono:** ‑á
- **my:** ‑áme
- **vy:** ‑áte
- **oni/ony:** ‑ajú

These endings are consistent across the class.

</section>

<section id="verb-examples">

### Example: _hľadať_ (“to search”)

Stem: **hľad‑**

- ja **hľadám**
- ty **hľadáš**
- on **hľadá**
- my **hľadáme**
- vy **hľadáte**
- oni **hľadajú**

### Example: _pýtať sa_ (“to ask”)

Stem: **pýt‑**

- ja **pýtam sa**
- ty **pýtaš sa**
- on **pýta sa**
- my **pýtame sa**
- vy **pýtate sa**
- oni **pýtajú sa**

</section>

<section id="verb-irregularities">

### Irregularities within the AT‑class

AT‑verbs are extremely regular.  
Only minor issues occur:

- Stress may shift (_pýtať_ → _pýtam_).
- Reflexive forms behave identically to non‑reflexive ones.
- A few verbs historically belonged to other classes but regularised into AT.

There are **no major stem irregularities**.

</section>

<section id="verb-notes">

### Notes and learner tips

- AT‑verbs are the most predictable Slovak verbs.
- Learners should master this class early.
- Endings are identical across almost all AT‑verbs.
- Reflexive forms (_pýtať sa_) follow the same pattern.

</section>
