# Verb Class: -sť (ST Verbs)

<section id="verb-overview">

ST‑verbs are Slovak verbs whose infinitive ends in **‑sť**.  
They form a distinctive class because the **‑sť** ending is removed and replaced with a consonant (often **d**) before adding present‑tense endings.

Common examples include:

- rásť — to grow
- znieť — to sound
- tiecť — to flow (historically related, now irregular)

Many verbs in this class show stem irregularities, but the endings remain stable.

</section>

<section id="verb-recognition">

### How to recognise ST‑verbs

A verb belongs to this class if:

- its infinitive ends in **‑sť**, and
- removing **‑sť** leaves a consonant‑final stem.

Examples:

- rásť → rast‑
- znieť → znie‑
- viecť (historical) → veď‑

</section>

<section id="verb-stem">

### How to derive the stem

**Regular heuristic rule:**

1. Remove **‑sť**
2. Add **‑d** to form the stem
3. Apply softening only if required by pronunciation

Example (regular heuristic):

- _blisť_ → remove _‑sť_ → _bli‑_ → add _d_ → **blid‑**

This pattern reflects a historical tendency to insert a dental consonant before endings.

**Irregular stem patterns inside the class**

Some verbs do not follow the “+d” rule:

- _rásť_ → **rast‑** (vowel alternation, no added _d_)
- _znieť_ → **znie‑** (stem unchanged)
- _tiecť_ → **teč‑** (fully irregular)

</section>

<section id="verb-endings">

### Present‑tense endings for ST‑verbs

- **ja:** ‑em
- **ty:** ‑eš
- **on/ona/ono:** ‑e
- **my:** ‑eme
- **vy:** ‑ete
- **oni/ony:** ‑u

These endings are consistent across the class.

</section>

<section id="verb-examples">

### Example (regular heuristic verb)

Let’s invent a regular ST‑verb: _blisť_ (“to glimmer”).

Stem: **blid‑**

- ja **blidem**
- ty **blideš**
- on **blide**
- my **blideme**
- vy **blidete**
- oni **blidu**

### Real ST‑verbs

**rásť → rast‑**

- ja **rastiem**
- ty **rastieš**
- on **rastie**
- my **rastieme**
- vy **rastiete**
- oni **rastú**

**znieť → znie‑**

- ja **zniem**
- ty **znieš**
- on **znie**
- my **znieme**
- vy **zniete**
- oni **znejú**

</section>

<section id="verb-irregularities">

### Irregularities within the ST‑class

- _rásť_ shows vowel alternation (_á → a_) and no added _d_.
- _znieť_ keeps its stem unchanged.
- _tiecť_ is historically related but now fully irregular.
- Some verbs insert _‑ie‑_ in the stem (_rast‑ie‑š_).
- Some verbs have parallel forms (_znejú_ vs _znú_).

</section>

<section id="verb-notes">

### Notes and learner tips

- The “add ‑d” rule is a helpful learning shortcut, not a strict rule.
- Many ST‑verbs are irregular; memorising the common ones helps.
- Endings are predictable — the stem is the part that varies.
- ST‑verbs often show vowel alternation or softening.

</section>
