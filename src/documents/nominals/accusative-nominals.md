# Accusative Case

Marks direct objects.  
Masculine animate nouns take genitive-style endings (explicitly shown below).

---

<section id="stems">

# Stems

<section id="adjective-stems">

### _Adjective stems_

- Adjectives keep their base stem (no dropping letters).
- Declension endings attach directly to the stem.
- Example: _dobr-_ → _dobrý, dobrého, dobrú_.

</section>

<section id="noun-stems">

### _Noun stems_

**Masculine**

- Consonant stems are kept.
- Nouns ending in **-ec** drop **e**.
  - Example: _chlapec → chlapc-_
- Nouns ending in **-a** drop **a**.
  - Example: _kolega → koleg-_

**Feminine**

- Nouns ending in **-a** drop **a** (_žena → žen-_).
- Nouns ending in **-ia** drop **ia** (_ulica → ulic-_).
- Consonant-ending feminine nouns remain unchanged (_dlaň_).

**Neuter**

- Follows nominative stem rules (_mesto → mest-_).

**Exceptions**

- Masculine animate in **-ec**: drop _e_.
- Masculine animate in **-a**: drop _a_.
- Feminine consonant stems remain unchanged.

</section>

</section>

<section id="endings">

## 2. Endings

<section id="noun-endings">

<section id="noun-endings-singular">

### **Noun endings — accusative singular**

**Masculine animate**

- Takes genitive singular endings: **-a**, **-u**
- Examples: _chlapca, muža, hrdinu_

**Masculine inanimate**

- Same as nominative singular
- Examples: _stroj, plán, hrad_

**Feminine**

- **-a → -u**
- **-ia → -iu**
- Consonant-ending nouns stay the same
- Examples: _ženu, krajinu, dlaň_

**Neuter**

- Same as nominative singular
- Examples: _mesto, auto, srdce_

</section>

<section id="noun-endings-plural">

### **Noun endings — accusative plural**

**Masculine animate**

- Takes genitive plural endings: **-ov**
- Examples: _chlapov, mužov_

**Masculine inanimate**

- Same as nominative plural
- Examples: _stroje, hrady, plány_

**Feminine**

- **-a → -y**
- **-ia → -ie**
- Consonant stems → **-i**
- Examples: _ženy, krajiny_

**Neuter**

- Same as nominative plural
- Examples: _mestá, autá, srdcia_

**Exceptions**

- Masculine animate in **-a** may take **-ov** (_kolegov_).
- Irregular feminine consonant stems may take **-e** (_dlaň → dlane_).

</section>
</section>

<section id="adjective-endings">

### **Adjective endings — accusative**

**Singular**

- Masculine animate → **-ého** (_dobrého chlapca_)
- Masculine inanimate → **-ý** (_dobrý dom_)
- Feminine → **-ú** (_dobrú ženu_)
- Neuter → **-é** (_dobré auto_)

**Plural**

- Masculine animate → **-ých** (_dobrých mužov_)
- Masculine inanimate → **-é** (_dobré domy_)
- Feminine → **-é** (_dobré knihy_)
- Neuter → **-é** (_dobré mestá_)

**Exceptions**

- Soft adjectives ending in **-í** keep long vowels (_cudzí → cudzieho_).

</section>

<section id="demonstrative-endings">

### Demonstrative endings — accusative

**Singular**

- Masculine animate → _toho_
- Masculine inanimate → _ten_
- Feminine → _tú_
- Neuter → _to_

**Plural**

- Masculine animate → _tých_
- Feminine → _tie_
- Neuter → _tie_

**Note**

- Patterns follow adjective endings; masculine animate mirrors genitive forms.

</section>

<section id="pronouns">

### Pronouns — accusative

**Singular**

- 1st: _mňa / ma_
- 2nd: _ťa / teba_
- 3rd masculine: _ho / neho_
- 3rd feminine: _ju / ňu_
- 3rd neuter: _ho / neho_

**Plural**

- 1st: _nás_
- 2nd: _vás_
- 3rd: _ich_

**Notes**

- Short forms (_ma, ťa, ho, ju_) used in speech and after verbs.
- Long forms (_mňa, teba, neho, ňu_) used for emphasis or after prepositions.

</section>

<section id="verbs">

### Frequently used verbs with accusative objects

Verbs that typically take an accusative object:

- _vidieť_ — to see (_Vidím dom / Vidím ho._)
- _mať_ — to have (_Mám knihu._)
- _poznať_ — to know a person (_Poznám Petra._)
- _čakať_ — to wait for (_Čakám autobus._)
- _hľadať_ — to look for (_Hľadám kľúče._)
- _milovať_ — to love (_Milujem hudbu._)
- _niesť_ — to carry (_Nesiem tašku._)
- _kupovať / kúpiť_ — to buy (_Kupujem chlieb._)
- _čítať_ — to read (_Čítam knihu._)
- _písať_ — to write (_Píšem list._)

**Notes**

- Some verbs also pair with prepositions (_čakať na + acc._).
- Pronouns frequently replace nouns (_Vidím ťa, Milujem ju_).

</section>

<section id="prepositions">

### Prepositions governing the accusative

These prepositions require the accusative:

- _na_ — onto, for (_Idem na stanicu._)
- _do_ (motion) — into (_Idem do školy._)
- _o_ — about (_Rozprávame o teba._)
- _po_ (motion) — after, around (_Idem po lekára._)
- _za_ (motion) — for, after (_Idem za prácou._)
- _medzi_ (motion) — among, between (_Sadol si medzi ľudí._)
- _cez_ — through (_Ideme cez park._)
- _pre_ — for (_Darček pre mamu._)

**Notes**

- Many also use other cases depending on meaning (_na, do, po, za, medzi_).
- Motion → **accusative**; location/state → **locative/instrumental**.

</section>

<section id="pronouns-introduction">

## **Accusative Pronouns Overview**

In Slovak, accusative pronouns indicate the **direct object** of a verb.  
They have **three forms**: short (unstressed), long (stressed/emphatic), and after-preposition.

Which form you use depends on:

- Sentence position
- Emphasis
- Presence of prepositions

This section explains all forms with examples.

</section>
<section id="pronouns-short-forms">

### **Accusative Pronouns — Short Forms (unstressed)**

Short forms are the **default accusative pronouns** used **immediately after a verb** in ordinary, unstressed speech.  
They are **not used after prepositions**.

**Key points:**

- Appear **early in the sentence**, directly after the verb.
- Used for **unstressed, neutral emphasis**.
- Cannot follow prepositions.

**Examples:**

- \*Vidím **ťa\*** — I see you
- \*Milujem **ju\*** — I love her
- \*Poznám **ho\*** — I know him
- _Mám **ma** doma._ — I have myself at home

**Common short forms (accusative singular/plural):**  
_ma, ťa, ho, ju, nás, vás, ich_

**Notes:**  
Even if you read only this section, remember: **short forms = unstressed, immediately after the verb, never after prepositions.**

</section>

<section id="pronouns-long-forms">

### **Accusative Pronouns — Long Forms (stressed or emphatic)**

Long forms are used for **emphasis, stress, or contrast** in a sentence.  
They can appear **later in the sentence** and clarify the object’s identity.

**Key points:**

- Can follow the verb or appear later.
- Used for **stressed or emphatic contexts**.
- Suitable for **formal, written, or spoken emphasis**.

**Examples:**

- _Vidím **teba**, nie jeho._ — I see **you**, not him
- _Poznám **mňa** lepšie než teba._ — I know myself better than you
- \*On hľadá **ju\*** — He is looking for **her** (neutral emphasis)

**Common long forms (accusative singular/plural):**  
_mňa, teba, jeho, ju, nás, vás, ich_

**Notes:**  
Even if read in isolation, know: **long forms = stressed/emphatic pronouns for direct objects in accusative.**

</section>

<section id="pronouns-after-prep">

### **Accusative Pronouns — After Preposition Forms**

Some accusative pronouns require a **special form after a preposition**, often starting with **n-**.  
These forms are mandatory whenever the pronoun follows a preposition that governs the accusative.

**Key points:**

- Short forms **cannot** be used after prepositions.
- Corresponds to `afterPrep` in code.
- Required for **grammatical correctness** in formal and spoken Slovak.

**Examples:**

- \*Pre **neho\*** — for him
- \*Cez **ňu\*** — through her
- \*Na **nich\*** — onto them

**Notes:**  
Even if read alone: **after-prep forms = required form of accusative pronoun following a preposition.**

</section>

<section id="pronouns-sentence-order">

### **Accusative Pronouns — Sentence Order and Usage Notes**

The choice of accusative pronoun form affects **sentence order**:

- **Short forms** appear **early**, clinging to the verb:  
  _Ja **ťa** vidím._ — I see you
- **Long forms** may appear **later** or be stressed:  
  \*Ja vidím **teba\*** — I see **you** (emphasis)
- **After-preposition forms** always follow the preposition:  
  _Pre **neho** som pripravený._ — I am ready for him

**Summary:**  
Even in isolation, remember:

- Short → unstressed, after verb
- Long → stressed/emphatic, flexible position
- After-prep → follows prepositions

</section>
