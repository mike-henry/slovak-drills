# Accusative Case
Marks direct objects.  
Masculine animate nouns take genitive-style endings (explicitly shown below).

---

<section id="stems">

# Stems

<section id="adjective-stems">

### *Adjective stems*
- Adjectives keep their base stem (no dropping letters).
- Declension endings attach directly to the stem.
- Example: *dobr-* → *dobrý, dobrého, dobrú*.

</section>

<section id="noun-stems">

### *Noun stems*

**Masculine**
- Consonant stems are kept.
- Nouns ending in **-ec** drop **e**.  
  - Example: *chlapec → chlapc-*
- Nouns ending in **-a** drop **a**.  
  - Example: *kolega → koleg-*

**Feminine**
- Nouns ending in **-a** drop **a** (*žena → žen-*).
- Nouns ending in **-ia** drop **ia** (*ulica → ulic-*).
- Consonant-ending feminine nouns remain unchanged (*dlaň*).

**Neuter**
- Follows nominative stem rules (*mesto → mest-*).

**Exceptions**
- Masculine animate in **-ec**: drop *e*.  
- Masculine animate in **-a**: drop *a*.  
- Feminine consonant stems remain unchanged.

</section>

</section>

<section id="endings">

## 2. Endings

<section id="noun-endings">

<section id="noun-endings-singular">

###  **Noun endings — accusative singular**

**Masculine animate**
- Takes genitive singular endings: **-a**, **-u**
- Examples: *chlapca, muža, hrdinu*

**Masculine inanimate**
- Same as nominative singular  
- Examples: *stroj, plán, hrad*

**Feminine**
- **-a → -u**  
- **-ia → -iu**  
- Consonant-ending nouns stay the same  
- Examples: *ženu, krajinu, dlaň*

**Neuter**
- Same as nominative singular  
- Examples: *mesto, auto, srdce*

</section>

<section id="noun-endings-plural">

### **Noun endings — accusative plural**

**Masculine animate**
- Takes genitive plural endings: **-ov**
- Examples: *chlapov, mužov*

**Masculine inanimate**
- Same as nominative plural  
- Examples: *stroje, hrady, plány*

**Feminine**
- **-a → -y**  
- **-ia → -ie**  
- Consonant stems → **-i**  
- Examples: *ženy, krajiny*

**Neuter**
- Same as nominative plural  
- Examples: *mestá, autá, srdcia*

**Exceptions**
- Masculine animate in **-a** may take **-ov** (*kolegov*).  
- Irregular feminine consonant stems may take **-e** (*dlaň → dlane*).

</section>
</section>

<section id="adjective-endings">

### **Adjective endings — accusative**

**Singular**
- Masculine animate → **-ého** (*dobrého chlapca*)
- Masculine inanimate → **-ý** (*dobrý dom*)
- Feminine → **-ú** (*dobrú ženu*)
- Neuter → **-é** (*dobré auto*)

**Plural**
- Masculine animate → **-ých** (*dobrých mužov*)
- Masculine inanimate → **-é** (*dobré domy*)
- Feminine → **-é** (*dobré knihy*)
- Neuter → **-é** (*dobré mestá*)

**Exceptions**
- Soft adjectives ending in **-í** keep long vowels (*cudzí → cudzieho*).

</section>

<section id="demonstrative-endings">

### Demonstrative endings — accusative

**Singular**
- Masculine animate → *toho*
- Masculine inanimate → *ten*
- Feminine → *tú*
- Neuter → *to*

**Plural**
- Masculine animate → *tých*
- Feminine → *tie*
- Neuter → *tie*

**Note**
- Patterns follow adjective endings; masculine animate mirrors genitive forms.

</section>

<section id="pronouns">

### Pronouns — accusative

**Singular**
- 1st: *mňa / ma*
- 2nd: *ťa / teba*
- 3rd masculine: *ho / neho*
- 3rd feminine: *ju / ňu*
- 3rd neuter: *ho / neho*

**Plural**
- 1st: *nás*
- 2nd: *vás*
- 3rd: *ich*

**Notes**
- Short forms (*ma, ťa, ho, ju*) used in speech and after verbs.
- Long forms (*mňa, teba, neho, ňu*) used for emphasis or after prepositions.

</section>

<section id="verbs">

### Frequently used verbs with accusative objects

Verbs that typically take an accusative object:
- *vidieť* — to see (*Vidím dom / Vidím ho.*)
- *mať* — to have (*Mám knihu.*)
- *poznať* — to know a person (*Poznám Petra.*)
- *čakať* — to wait for (*Čakám autobus.*)
- *hľadať* — to look for (*Hľadám kľúče.*)
- *milovať* — to love (*Milujem hudbu.*)
- *niesť* — to carry (*Nesiem tašku.*)
- *kupovať / kúpiť* — to buy (*Kupujem chlieb.*)
- *čítať* — to read (*Čítam knihu.*)
- *písať* — to write (*Píšem list.*)

**Notes**
- Some verbs also pair with prepositions (*čakať na + acc.*).
- Pronouns frequently replace nouns (*Vidím ťa, Milujem ju*).

</section>

<section id="prepositions">

### Prepositions governing the accusative

These prepositions require the accusative:
- *na* — onto, for (*Idem na stanicu.*)
- *do* (motion) — into (*Idem do školy.*)
- *o* — about (*Rozprávame o teba.*)
- *po* (motion) — after, around (*Idem po lekára.*)
- *za* (motion) — for, after (*Idem za prácou.*)
- *medzi* (motion) — among, between (*Sadol si medzi ľudí.*)
- *cez* — through (*Ideme cez park.*)
- *pre* — for (*Darček pre mamu.*)

**Notes**
- Many also use other cases depending on meaning (*na, do, po, za, medzi*).
- Motion → **accusative**; location/state → **locative/instrumental**.
