# Instrumental Case

Used to express **the means**, **the companion**, or **the tool** used to perform an action.  
Often used after verbs meaning _working with_, _going with_, _dealing with_, and with prepositions like **s/so, nad, pod, pred, za** (when static).

---

</section>
<section id="stems">

## 1. Stems

<section id="adjective-stems">

### Adjective stems

- Adjectives keep their base stem.
- Endings attach directly to the stem.
- Example: _dobr-_ → _dobrým, dobrou_.

</section>

<section id="noun-stems">

### Noun stems

**Masculine**

- Consonant stems stay unchanged.
- Nouns in **-ec** drop _e_: _chlapec → chlapc-_.
- Nouns in **-a** drop _a_: _kolega → koleg-_.

**Feminine**

- **-a** → drop _a_ (_žena → žen-_).
- **-ia** → drop _ia_ (_kaviareň → kaviar-_).
- Consonant stems unchanged (_dlaň → dlaň-_).

**Neuter**

- Same rules as nominative stems.
- _mesto → mest-_.

**Exceptions**

- Masculine animate **-ec** and **-a** behave irregularly.
- Feminine consonant stems remain unchanged.

</section>

</section>

## 2. Endings

<section id="noun-endings">

<section id="noun-endings-singular">

### Noun endings — instrumental singular

**Masculine**

- Ending **-om**  
  Examples: \*s chlapc**om\***, \*s muž**om\***.

**Feminine**

- **-ou** for most feminine nouns  
  Examples: \*so žen**ou\***, \*s kraj**inou\***.

**Neuter**

- **-om**  
  Examples: \*s mest**om\***, \*so srdc**om\***.

</section>

<section id="noun-endings-plural">

### Noun endings — instrumental plural

**Masculine**

- **-mi**  
  Example: \*s chlap**mi\***.

**Feminine**

- **-ami**  
  Example: \*so žen**ami\***.

**Neuter**

- **-ami**  
  Example: \*s mest**ami\***.

**Notes**

- Neuters follow the feminine plural pattern.
- Masculine animate/inanimate share the same ending.

</section>

</section>

<section id="adjective-endings">

### Adjective endings — instrumental

**Singular**

- Masculine: **-ým** → _s dobr**ým** mužom_
- Feminine: **-ou** → _s dobr**ou** ženou_
- Neuter: **-ým** → _s dobr**ým** mestom_

**Plural**

- All genders: **-ými**  
  Example: _s dobr**ými** ľuďmi_

**Notes:**

- Soft adjectives in **-í** follow the same pattern (\*cudz**ím\***, \*cudz**ou\***).

</section>

<section id="demonstrative-endings">

### Demonstrative endings — instrumental

**Singular**

- Masculine: \*s t**ým\***
- Feminine: \*s t**ou\***
- Neuter: \*s t**ým\***

**Plural**

- All genders: \*s t**ými\***.

</section>

<section id="pronouns">

### Pronouns — instrumental

**Singular**

- 1st: _so mnou_
- 2nd: _s tebou_
- 3rd M: _s ním_
- 3rd F: _s ňou_
- 3rd N: _s ním_

**Plural**

- 1st: _s nami_
- 2nd: _s vami_
- 3rd: _s nimi_

**Notes:**

- _so_ is used before certain consonant clusters.

</section>

<section id="verbs">

### Frequently used verbs with instrumental objects

These verbs often use the instrumental (usually with the preposition **s/so**):

- _pracovať s_ — to work with  
  _Pracujem s počítačom._
- _stretnúť sa s_ — to meet with  
  _Stretol som sa s kamarátom._
- _zaoberať sa_ — to deal with  
  _Zaoberám sa projektom._
- _manipulovať s_ — to handle  
  _Manipulujem s nástrojmi._
- _hýbať (niečím)_ — to move (something)  
  _Hýbem rukami._

**Notes:**

- Instrumental expresses **companionship**, **tool**, or **method**.

</section>

<section id="prepositions">

### Prepositions governing the instrumental

The instrumental is required after these prepositions:

- **s / so** — with  
  _S kamarátom, so sestrou._
- **pred** — in front of  
  _Pred domom._
- **za** — behind  
  _Za školou._
- **nad** — above  
  _Nad stolom._
- **pod** — under  
  _Pod mostom._
- **medzi** — among  
  _Medzi ľuďmi._

**Notes / exceptions:**

- These same prepositions use **accusative** when expressing **motion**.
- Instrumental expresses **static location**, **accompaniment**, or **means**.

</section>

## **Instrumental Pronouns Overview**

In Slovak, instrumental pronouns indicate the **companion, means, or tool** used to perform an action.  
They are always used **with the preposition `s` or `so`** (except some set expressions).

Instrumental pronouns have **three forms**:

- Short (unstressed)
- Long (stressed/emphatic)
- After-preposition (required after prepositions)

Which form you use depends on:

- Emphasis
- Sentence position
- Formality

</section>

<section id="pronouns-short-forms">

### **Instrumental Pronouns — Short Forms (unstressed)**

Short forms are **unstressed pronouns** used with **`s/so` in everyday speech**.  
They appear immediately after the preposition in neutral, casual contexts.

**Key points:**

- Used in ordinary, unstressed speech
- Follow the preposition `s`/`so`
- Not suitable for emphasis

**Examples:**

- \*Pracujem **s mnou\*** — I work with myself
- \*Stretol som sa **s tebou\*** — I met with you
- \*Manipulujem **s ním\*** — I handle it (him)

**Notes:**  
Even if this section is read alone: **short forms = neutral, immediately after `s/so`, unstressed.**

</section>

<section id="pronouns-long-forms">

### **Instrumental Pronouns — Long Forms (stressed or emphatic)**

Long forms are used for **stress, contrast, or emphasis**.  
They can appear **later in the sentence** or in formal writing.

**Key points:**

- Flexible placement in the sentence
- Emphasizes the object or companion
- Always used **with `s`/`so`**

**Examples:**

- _Stretol som sa **so mnou**, nie s ním._ — I met with **me**, not him
- _Pracujem **so sebou** každý deň._ — I work **with myself** every day (emphatic)

**Notes:**  
Long forms = stressed/emphatic pronouns for instrumental objects.

</section>

<section id="pronouns-after-prep">

### **Instrumental Pronouns — After Preposition Forms**

In instrumental, this form is **mandatory after prepositions** like `s`, `so`, `nad`, `pod`, `za`, `pred`, `medzi`.

**Key points:**

- Short forms cannot be used after prepositions in formal or written contexts
- Follows the preposition directly
- Required for **grammatical correctness**

**Examples:**

- \*S **ním\*** — with him
- \*So **ňou\*** — with her
- \*Medzi **nimi\*** — among them

**Notes:**  
Even read in isolation: **after-prep forms = required when using a preposition governing the instrumental.**

</section>

<section id="pronouns-sentence-order">

### **Instrumental Pronouns — Sentence Order and Usage Notes**

Sentence order depends on the pronoun form:

- **Short forms:** immediately follow the preposition  
  \*Pracujem s **mnou\*** — neutral, unstressed
- **Long forms:** appear later or for emphasis  
  _Pracujem s **mnou**, nie s ním._ — I work with **me**, not him
- **After-prep forms:** always follow prepositions  
  _Medzi **nimi** sedeli pri stole._ — Among **them**, they sat at the table

**Summary:**

- Short → unstressed, casual, directly after preposition
- Long → stressed/emphatic, flexible placement
- After-prep → mandatory after prepositions
